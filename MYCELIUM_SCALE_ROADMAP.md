## SCALING RULES

### Cell Rules
1. **Never exceed 13 members** - Split when larger
2. **Minimum 3 members** - Merge if smaller
3. **Regular contribution required** - Active or archived
4. **Charter required** - Written agreements
5. **Exit always possible** - No coercion

### Hub Rules
1. **Coordinate 5-15 cells max** - Split if larger
2. **Rotating leadership** - 6-month terms max
3. **No authority over cells** - Coordination only
4. **Transparent operations** - All decisions visible
5. **Emergency protocols** - Clear escalation

### Network Rules
1. **Protocol, not platform** - Can always fork
2. **Identity portable** - Your data is yours
3. **Trust earned, not granted** - Verify everything
4. **Contribution required** - No pure consumers
5. **Physical and digital** - Both required

## RISK MITIGATION

### Risk: Platform Capture
**Scenario:** Single entity gains control of infrastructure
**Mitigation:**
- Protocol-based, not platform-based
- Multiple implementations encouraged
- Data export always available
- Forking explicitly supported

### Risk: Hierarchy Emergence
**Scenario:** Some nodes accumulate power
**Mitigation:**
- Mandatory term limits
- Contribution requirements for all levels
- Trust decay over time
- Cell-level veto power

### Risk: Free Riders
**Scenario:** Taking without contributing
**Mitigation:**
- Contribution tracking
- Access tied to participation
- Credit expiration
- Cell-level accountability

### Risk: Bad Actors
**Scenario:** Malicious participants
**Mitigation:**
- Vouching required for entry
- Trust levels gate access
- Reputation is public
- Cells can exclude
- Network can isolate

### Risk: Growth Too Fast
**Scenario:** Quality suffers from quantity
**Mitigation:**
- Cell size limits
- Vouching bottlenecks
- Trust level requirements
- Organic splitting only
